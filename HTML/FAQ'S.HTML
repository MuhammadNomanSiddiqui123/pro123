To enter numbers in scientific notation, use the format: a x 10^b, where a is a number between 1 and 10, and b is an integer. For example, to enter 3000, you would input 3 x 10^3.
A knot is a unit of speed equal to one nautical mile per hour, commonly used in maritime and aviation contexts. A nautical mile is approximately 1.15078 miles or 1.852 kilometers, based on the circumference of the Earth.
To convert inches of rain to millimeters, multiply the number of inches by 25.4. For example, 1 inch of rain is equivalent to 25.4 millimeters. Conversely, to convert millimeters to inches, divide the number of millimeters by 25.4.
A long ton (also known as an imperial ton) is equal to 1,016 kilograms (2,240 pounds). A short ton (also known as a US ton) is equal to 907.185 kilograms (2,000 pounds). A metric ton (tonne) is equal to 1,000 kilograms (2,204.62 pounds). Each ton is used in different contexts depending on the country and measurement system.
Celsius and Fahrenheit are the same at -40 degrees. At this temperature, both scales indicate the same value, making it a unique point of reference.
To convert a matrix from row to column format (transpose), swap the rows with columns. For instance, if you have a matrix: [1, 2] in row format, its column format will be: [1]
[2].
The determinant of a matrix is a scalar value that can be computed from its elements. For a 2x2 matrix [[a, b], [c, d]], the determinant is calculated as ad - bc. For larger matrices, the calculation involves more complex methods like row reduction or the cofactor expansion.
To add or subtract matrices, they must have the same dimensions. Simply add or subtract corresponding elements. For example, for matrices A = [[a, b], [c, d]] and B = [[e, f], [g, h]], their sum is A + B = [[a+e, b+f], [c+g, d+h]].
Matrix multiplication involves taking the dot product of rows from the first matrix with columns of the second. The number of columns in the first matrix must equal the number of rows in the second. For matrices A (m x n) and B (n x p), the result is a new matrix C (m x p).
The identity matrix is a square matrix with ones on the diagonal and zeros elsewhere. For example, the 2x2 identity matrix is [[1, 0], [0, 1]]. When any matrix is multiplied by the identity matrix, the original matrix remains unchanged.
To find the inverse of a matrix A, it must be square and its determinant must not be zero. For a 2x2 matrix [[a, b], [c, d]], the inverse is given by (1/det(A)) * [[d, -b], [-c, a]], where det(A) = ad - bc. For larger matrices, methods such as row reduction or adjugate calculation can be used.
Eigenvalues are scalars associated with a linear transformation represented by a matrix. An eigenvector is a non-zero vector that changes only by a scalar factor when that transformation is applied. Mathematically, A * v = λ * v, where λ is the eigenvalue and v is the eigenvector.
To convert a matrix to reduced row echelon form (RREF), perform a series of row operations (swapping, scaling, and adding rows) until each leading entry is 1, and all entries in the column above and below each leading entry are zeros. This process is often used to solve systems of linear equations.
