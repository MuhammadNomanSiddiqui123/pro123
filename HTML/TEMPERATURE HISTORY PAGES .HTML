Temperature Conversions

This section discusses the importance of temperature conversions and provides formulas for common conversions.

Fahrenheit to Celsius

The formula to convert Fahrenheit to Celsius is:

C = (F - 32) × 5/9

Conversely, to convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, use:

F = C × 9/5 + 32

Examples

Here are some examples of the conversions:

  • Example 1: Convert 68°F to Celsius:

    • C = (68 - 32) × 5/9 = 20°C

  • Example 2: Convert 100°C to Fahrenheit:

    • F = 100 × 9/5 + 32 = 212°F

Conclusion

Understanding temperature conversions is essential for various applications, including cooking, science, and weather reporting.
